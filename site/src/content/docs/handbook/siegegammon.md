SiegeGammon flips backgammon on its head: instead of bearing off, players deploy checkers from reserve onto an empty board and race to lock all 15 into the opponent's home territory.

## How it works

The game starts with an empty board. Players take turns:

1. **Deploy** checkers from reserve onto the board
2. **Advance** checkers toward the opponent's home territory
3. **Lock** checkers into the Siege Zone to score

## Spatial inversion

The board layout creates natural tension: your Garrison (home area) overlaps the opponent's Siege Zone (target area). This means every checker you deploy to defend is also a checker your opponent is trying to get past.

Contact and tension stay high from the first move to the last — there's no quiet "bearing off" endgame.

## Scoring multipliers

| Multiplier | Condition | Effect |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| Standard | Normal win | 1x stakes |
| Siege | Lock all 15 before opponent locks 8 | 2x stakes |
| Total Siege | Lock all 15 before opponent locks any | 3x stakes |

The doubling cube from standard backgammon is available for high-stakes games.
